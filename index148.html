BIBLE QUIZ: NO FAVORITISM WITH GOD

Colossians 3:25
30
According to Colossians 3:25, anyone who does wrong will be repaid for their wrong, and there is no ______.
Forgiveness
Favoritism
Excuse
Mercy

Correct Answer: Favoritism (Colossians 3:25)

Colossians 3:25 (NIV): "Anyone who does wrong will be repaid for their wrong, and there is no favoritism."

God's Perfect Justice:

  1. Divine Accountability: Every wrong will be repaid
  2. Impartial Judgment: God shows no partiality
  3. Universal Standard: Same rules apply to everyone
  4. Moral Order: Justice is built into creation

Context in Colossians 3:22-25:

  • Instructions to slaves about serving their earthly masters
  • Encouragement to work with sincerity and reverence for the Lord
  • Promise of inheritance as reward for serving Christ
  • Warning that wrongdoers will face consequences
  • Emphasis on God's impartial judgment system

What "No Favoritism" Means:

  • Equal Standards: Rich and poor judged by same measure
  • No Special Treatment: Status or position doesn't exempt anyone
  • Pure Justice: Decisions based on truth, not preferences
  • Universal Application: Applies to all people in all situations
  • Divine Character: Reflects God's nature of perfect justice

Biblical Examples of God's Impartiality:

  • Peter's Revelation: "God shows no favoritism" (Acts 10:34)
  • James' Warning: Don't show favoritism to the rich (James 2:1-9)
  • Paul's Teaching: God judges everyone equally (Romans 2:11)
  • Old Testament: "Do not pervert justice" (Leviticus 19:15)

Practical Implications for Believers:

  • Personal Responsibility: We're accountable for our actions
  • Fair Treatment: Should reflect God's impartiality in relationships
  • Moral Courage: Do right even when no one is watching
  • Trust in Justice: Believe God will ultimately make things right
  • Humility: No one gets special treatment before God

The Balance of Justice and Mercy:

  • Justice Demanded: Wrong must be accounted for
  • Mercy Provided: Through Christ's sacrifice
  • Personal Choice: Accept mercy or face justice
  • Eternal Perspective: Choices have eternal consequences
  • Gospel Hope: Christ bore our punishment

Modern Applications:

  • Workplace Ethics: Treat all colleagues fairly
  • Church Leadership: Avoid showing partiality
  • Personal Relationships: Judge people by character, not status
  • Social Justice: Advocate for fair treatment of all
  • Self-Examination: Check our own biases and preferences

Key Insight: God's perfect justice means everyone will face consequences for their wrongs without exception or favoritism. This truth should inspire both holy fear and deep gratitude for the mercy available through Jesus Christ, who bore our punishment so we could receive grace instead of justice.
