BIBLE QUIZ: JESUS' CIRCUMCISION & NAMING

Luke 2:21
30
According to Luke 2:21, when was baby Jesus circumcised and given the name Jesus?
On the night of His birth (with shepherds present)
On the 8th day after His birth
At 40 days old (during temple presentation)
At 1 year old (when Wise Men visited)

Correct Answer: On the 8th day after His birth (Luke 2:21)

Luke 2:21 (NIV): "On the eighth day, when it was time to circumcise the child, he was named Jesus, the name the angel had given him before he was conceived."

Biblical Command for 8th Day Circumcision:

  • Genesis 17:12: "For the generations to come every male among you who is eight days old must be circumcised."
  • Leviticus 12:3: "On the eighth day the boy is to be circumcised."
  • Covenant Sign: Circumcision as sign of Abrahamic covenant (Genesis 17:11)

Complete Timeline of Jesus' First 40 Days:

  1. Day 1 (Birth): Born in Bethlehem, laid in manger (Luke 2:6-7)
  2. Same Night: Shepherds visit and spread the news (Luke 2:8-20)
  3. Day 8: Circumcised and officially named Jesus (Luke 2:21)
  4. Day 40: Presented at Jerusalem Temple (Luke 2:22-24)
  5. Day 40: Simeon's prophecy and Anna's testimony (Luke 2:25-38)

Why the 8th Day? Medical & Theological Significance:

  • Vitamin K Peak: Blood clotting factor Vitamin K peaks on 8th day
  • Prothrombin Levels: Highest on 8th day (110% of normal)
  • Divine Wisdom: God's command aligned with perfect biological timing
  • Healing: Quickest healing at this developmental stage

The Name "Jesus" - Meaning & Significance:

  • Hebrew: Yeshua/Yehoshua (יֵשׁוּעַ/יְהוֹשֻׁעַ)
  • Meaning: "Yahweh saves" or "The LORD is salvation"
  • Angel's Announcement to Mary: "You are to call him Jesus" (Luke 1:31)
  • Angel's Explanation to Joseph: "You are to give him the name Jesus, because he will save his people from their sins" (Matthew 1:21)

Jewish Customs for Circumcision & Naming (Brit Milah):

  • Mohel: Specially trained circumciser
  • Sandek: Godfather who holds baby during ceremony
  • Blessings: Specific prayers and blessings recited
  • Naming: Formal announcement of child's Hebrew name
  • Celebration: Festive meal (seudat mitzvah) following ceremony

Theological Significance of Jesus' Circumcision:

  • Law Fulfillment: Jesus submitted to Jewish Law from infancy
  • Covenant Identity: Identified with Abraham's descendants
  • Redemptive Work: "Born under the law to redeem those under the law" (Galatians 4:4-5)
  • True Israel: Represented perfect covenant obedience
  • Spiritual Circumcision: Pointed to circumcision of the heart (Romans 2:29)

What Happened at the Temple on Day 40?

  • Purification Offering: Mary's purification after childbirth (Leviticus 12:6-8)
  • Poor Person's Offering: "A pair of doves or two young pigeons" (Luke 2:24)
  • Firstborn Redemption: Jesus presented as firstborn (Exodus 13:2)
  • Simeon's Recognition: Recognized Jesus as Messiah (Luke 2:25-35)
  • Anna's Proclamation: Spoke about Jesus to all awaiting redemption (Luke 2:36-38)

Important Distinctions in Timing:

  • Circumcision vs Presentation: Different events (Day 8 vs Day 40)
  • Naming vs Registration: Named at circumcision, not at census
  • Shepherds vs Simeon: Different groups witnessed different events
  • Bethlehem vs Jerusalem: Different locations for different ceremonies

Cultural Context of First-Century Judea:

  • Family Celebration: Circumcision important family/community event
  • Religious Obligation: Commandment taken seriously by devout Jews
  • Covenant Commitment: Sign of belonging to God's covenant people
  • Prophetic Expectation: Messiah expected to fulfill all Law perfectly

New Testament Fulfillment & Transformation:

  • Colossians 2:11-12: Spiritual circumcision through Christ
  • Romans 2:28-29: True circumcision is of the heart
  • Philippians 3:3: We worship by Spirit, not fleshly circumcision
  • Acts 15: Circumcision not required for Gentile believers
  • Galatians 5:6: What counts is faith expressing through love

Key Insight: Baby Jesus was circumcised and officially named "Jesus" on the 8th day after His birth, in perfect obedience to the Mosaic Law (Genesis 17:12, Leviticus 12:3). This event demonstrated His full identification with the Jewish people and His submission to God's covenant from the very beginning of His earthly life. The name "Jesus" (Yeshua - "The LORD saves") was divinely appointed and announced by angels before His conception, perfectly reflecting His mission to save His people from their sins. This early obedience foreshadowed His complete fulfillment of the Law throughout His life and ministry.
