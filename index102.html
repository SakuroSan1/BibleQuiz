BIBLE QUIZ: TRANSFORMATION IN CHRIST

Philemon 1:15-16
30
According to Philemon, Onesimus was no longer just a slave but now a ______.
Free man
Brother in Christ
Paid servant
Business partner

Correct Answer: Brother in Christ (Philemon 1:16)

Philemon 1:15-16 (NIV): "Perhaps the reason he was separated from you for a little while was that you might have him back forever—no longer as a slave, but better than a slave, as a dear brother."

The Gospel Transformation:

  • Before Christ: Onesimus was a runaway slave (v.15)
  • After Conversion: Became a "dear brother" in Christ (v.16)
  • Relationship Change: Spiritual family bond supersedes social status
  • Paul's Mediation: Appeals based on Christian love, not law

Key Principles:

  • Reconciliation: The gospel restores broken relationships
  • Equality in Christ: Social status becomes secondary to spiritual unity (Galatians 3:28)
  • Voluntary Love: Paul appeals rather than commands (v.8-9)
  • Eternal Perspective: "Have him back forever" (v.15)

Practical Applications:

  • The gospel breaks down social barriers
  • Christian relationships should be based on love, not status
  • Forgiveness and restoration are central to Christian faith
  • We are called to see others as Christ sees them

Modern Relevance: This letter challenges us to examine how the gospel should transform our relationships across social, economic, and racial divides.
