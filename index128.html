BIBLE QUIZ: THE PROPER USE OF LAW

1 Timothy 1:8
30
According to 1 Timothy 1:8, "We know that the law is good if one uses it ______."
Properly
Strictly
Legally
Wisely

Correct Answer: Properly (1 Timothy 1:8)

1 Timothy 1:8 (NIV): "We know that the law is good if one uses it properly."

The Purpose and Proper Use of God's Law:

  1. Reveals Sin: Shows us our need for salvation (Romans 3:20)
  2. Leads to Christ: Acts as a tutor to bring us to Christ (Galatians 3:24)
  3. Reflects God's Character: Reveals God's holiness and standards
  4. Guides Christian Living: Provides moral guidance for believers

Improper Uses of the Law (1 Timothy 1:6-7):

  • Promoting meaningless talk and speculation
  • Teaching false doctrines
  • Emphasizing genealogies and myths
  • Using it for legalistic control rather than spiritual growth

Proper vs Improper Use of Law:

  • PROPER: To convict of sin and point to Christ
  • IMPROPER: To establish self-righteousness
  • PROPER: To guide in righteous living
  • IMPROPER: To burden with impossible requirements
  • PROPER: To reveal God's character
  • IMPROPER: To promote legalistic traditions

Biblical Context (1 Timothy 1:8-11):

  • Paul contrasts proper and improper use of law
  • Law is made for lawbreakers and rebels
  • It condemns various sins listed in verses 9-10
  • Proper use aligns with the gospel of Christ

Practical Applications:

  • Humility: Recognizing we all fall short of God's law
  • Grace-Focused: Using law to magnify God's grace
  • Christ-Centered: Seeing Christ as the fulfillment of the law
  • Freedom: Living by the Spirit, not legalistic bondage

Key Insight: The law is like a mirror that shows us our dirt but cannot wash it away - that requires the cleansing blood of Christ.
