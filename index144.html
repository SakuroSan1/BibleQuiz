BIBLE QUIZ: GOSPEL CAME WITH POWER

1 Thessalonians 1:5
30
According to 1 Thessalonians 1:5, the gospel came to you not simply with words but also with ______.
Power
Wisdom
Love
Prophecy

Correct Answer: Power (1 Thessalonians 1:5)

1 Thessalonians 1:5 (NIV): "because our gospel came to you not simply with words but also with power, with the Holy Spirit and deep conviction."

The Multi-Dimensional Gospel:

  1. Beyond Words: The gospel transcends human eloquence
  2. Divine Power: Accompanied by supernatural demonstration
  3. Spiritual Presence: Empowered by the Holy Spirit
  4. Heart Transformation: Produces deep conviction and change

Context in 1 Thessalonians 1:4-6:

  • Paul reminds the Thessalonians of their election by God
  • The gospel came with power despite persecution
  • They received the message with joy given by the Holy Spirit
  • They became imitators of Paul and of the Lord
  • Their faith became known throughout the region

What "Power" Means in This Context:

  • Miraculous Demonstration: Signs and wonders confirming the message
  • Conviction Power: Ability to penetrate hardened hearts
  • Transforming Power: Changes lives from the inside out
  • Sustaining Power: Enables endurance through trials
  • Reproducing Power: Spreads rapidly and effectively

The Threefold Manifestation:

  • Power: Divine energy and miraculous works
  • Holy Spirit: God's personal presence and guidance
  • Deep Conviction: Certainty and assurance in believers

Biblical Examples of Gospel Power:

  • Pentecost: Holy Spirit's power transforming disciples (Acts 2)
  • Paul's Ministry: Signs and wonders confirming his message (Romans 15:18-19)
  • Hebrews 4:12: Word of God is living, active, and powerful
  • Romans 1:16: Gospel is the power of God for salvation

Practical Applications for Today:

  • Dependence on Spirit: Rely on Holy Spirit rather than human ability
  • Expect Miracles: Believe God works supernaturally today
  • Pray for Power: Ask God to demonstrate His power in ministry
  • Share Confidently: Trust the gospel's inherent power to transform
  • Seek Conviction: Pray for deep heart transformation in listeners

Evidence of Gospel Power in Believers:

  • Changed Lives: Transformation of character and priorities
  • Spiritual Gifts: Manifestation of Holy Spirit's gifts
  • Bold Witness: Courage to share faith despite opposition
  • Enduring Faith: Perseverance through trials and persecution
  • Joyful Service: Willingness to serve with joy and sacrifice

Key Insight: The true gospel is never merely a human message but always comes with divine power that transforms lives, confirms truth, and demonstrates God's reality through the Holy Spirit's work in and through believers.
