BIBLE QUIZ: JESUS CALLED JUSTUS

Colossians 4:11
30
According to Colossians 4:11, Justus, the fellow prisoner who is also called ______.
Jesus
Barnabas
Silas
Barsabbas

Correct Answer: Jesus (Colossians 4:11)

Colossians 4:11 (NIV): "Jesus, who is called Justus, also sends greetings. These are the only Jews among my co-workers for the kingdom of God, and they have proved a comfort to me."

The Faithful Companion:

  1. Dual Identity: Known as both Jesus and Justus
  2. Loyal Co-worker: One of Paul's trusted companions
  3. Jewish Background: Among the few Jewish helpers
  4. Prison Ministry: Stood with Paul during imprisonment

Context in Colossians 4:7-14:

  • Paul sends greetings from his companions in Rome
  • Lists faithful co-workers who comforted him
  • Jesus called Justus is mentioned among Jewish helpers
  • They worked together for the kingdom of God
  • Demonstrated unity across cultural backgrounds

Significance of Jesus Called Justus:

  • Name Meaning: "Jesus" means "Yahweh saves," "Justus" means "righteous"
  • Cultural Bridge: Jewish believer serving with Gentile churches
  • Faithful Support: Provided comfort during Paul's imprisonment
  • Team Ministry: Part of Paul's inner circle of workers
  • Historical Witness: Early church leader mentioned in Scripture

The Three Jewish Co-workers:

  • Aristarchus: Fellow prisoner and traveling companion
  • Mark: Cousin of Barnabas, later reconciled with Paul
  • Jesus called Justus: Faithful comfort to Paul in prison

Biblical Significance of Names:

  • Jesus: Common Jewish name (Joshua in Hebrew)
  • Justus: Latin name meaning "righteous" or "just"
  • Dual Names: Common practice in multicultural Roman world
  • Identity: Maintained both Jewish and Roman identities

Lessons from Jesus Called Justus:

  • Faithful Service: Ministry isn't always in the spotlight
  • Cross-cultural Ministry: Serving beyond one's own background
  • Prison Ministry: Standing with those in difficult circumstances
  • Teamwork: Essential for effective kingdom work
  • Comfort Ministry: Providing emotional and spiritual support

Modern Applications:

  • Support Roles: Valuing behind-the-scenes ministry
  • Cultural Sensitivity: Working across different backgrounds
  • Prison Ministry: Visiting and supporting incarcerated believers
  • Encouragement: Being a comfort to leaders and pastors
  • Faithfulness: Consistent service regardless of recognition

Key Insight: Jesus called Justus represents the often-unsung heroes of ministry - faithful companions who provide comfort, support kingdom work across cultural boundaries, and demonstrate that true service doesn't require public recognition but steadfast faithfulness in challenging circumstances.
