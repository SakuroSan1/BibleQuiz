BIBLE QUIZ: ANGER MANAGEMENT

Ephesians 4:26
30
According to Ephesians 4:26, "In your anger do not ______."
Sin
Yell
Fight
Hate

Correct Answer: Sin (Ephesians 4:26)

Ephesians 4:26 (NIV): "In your anger do not sin: Do not let the sun go down while you are still angry."

Key Truths About Anger:

  1. Anger is not sin: It's an emotion God created
  2. Anger can lead to sin: When mismanaged or held too long
  3. Time limit: Don't let sun go down on your anger
  4. Resolution required: Deal with anger promptly

The Full Verse & Context:

  • Ephesians 4:26-27: "In your anger do not sin: Do not let the sun go down while you are still angry, and do not give the devil a foothold."
  • Connected to Ephesians 4:25-32: Part of instructions for Christian living
  • Context: Speaking truth, controlling anger, honest work, helpful speech

Three Types of Anger in Scripture:

  • Righteous Anger: Anger at sin/injustice (Jesus cleansing temple - John 2:13-17)
  • Sinful Anger: Self-centered, uncontrolled anger (Cain - Genesis 4:5-8)
  • Slow-burning Anger: Harbored resentment (Jonah - Jonah 4:1-4)

Why "Don't Let the Sun Go Down":

  • Prevents bitterness: Unresolved anger turns to bitterness
  • Protects relationships: Prompt reconciliation
  • Guards heart: Anger affects sleep and health
  • Spiritual protection: Prevents devil's foothold

Biblical Examples of Anger Management:

  • Jesus: Righteous anger at temple corruption (Matthew 21:12-13)
  • Moses: Sinful anger when striking rock (Numbers 20:10-12)
  • David: Controlled anger toward Nabal (1 Samuel 25:32-34)
  • Joseph: Forgiveness instead of anger toward brothers (Genesis 45:4-8)

Practical Steps for Godly Anger Management:

  • Pause & Pray: Before reacting (Psalm 4:4)
  • Identify Root Cause: What's really bothering you?
  • Express Appropriately: Use "I feel" statements
  • Seek Resolution: Before day ends (Matthew 5:23-24)
  • Forgive: As God forgave you (Ephesians 4:32)

What Anger Reveals About Our Hearts:

  • Idols: Anger often signals something we worship besides God
  • Expectations: Unmet expectations lead to frustration
  • Insecurity: Anger as defense mechanism
  • Pride: Thinking we deserve better treatment

Ephesians 4:26 in Greek:

  • "Orgizesthe": Present imperative - "Be angry" (it's a command!)
  • "Kai": "And" or "But" - connective
  • "Me hamartanete": "Do not sin" - present imperative negative
  • Significance: Anger itself is permitted, but sinning in anger is forbidden

Related Scriptures on Anger:

  • James 1:19-20: "Everyone should be quick to listen, slow to speak and slow to become angry, because human anger does not produce the righteousness that God desires."
  • Proverbs 29:11: "Fools give full vent to their rage, but the wise bring calm in the end."
  • Proverbs 15:1: "A gentle answer turns away wrath, but a harsh word stirs up anger."
  • Ecclesiastes 7:9: "Do not be quickly provoked in your spirit, for anger resides in the lap of fools."
  • Colossians 3:8: "But now you must also rid yourselves of all such things as these: anger, rage, malice..."

The Danger of Unresolved Anger:

  • Gives devil foothold: Ephesians 4:27 warning
  • Leads to bitterness: Hebrews 12:15
  • Affects prayer: Mark 11:25
  • Damages relationships: Proverbs 22:24-25
  • Harms health: Proverbs 14:30

Key Insight: Ephesians 4:26 doesn't say "Don't be angry." It says "In your anger do not sin." This recognizes anger as a legitimate human emotion that God created. The key is managing anger righteously - expressing it appropriately, dealing with it promptly (before sunset), and not allowing it to fester into sin. This verse, along with the warning about giving the devil a foothold in verse 27, shows that properly managed anger is part of healthy Christian living, while mismanaged anger opens doors to spiritual attack and relational destruction.
