Correct Answer: Judge (Colossians 2:16)

Colossians 2:16 (NIV): "Therefore do not let anyone judge you by what you eat or drink, or with regard to a religious festival, a New Moon celebration or a Sabbath day."

Christian Freedom from Legalism:

  1. External Observances: Food, drink, festivals, Sabbaths
  2. Freedom in Christ: Not bound by ceremonial laws
  3. Personal Conviction: Matters of individual conscience
  4. Spiritual Reality: These were shadows pointing to Christ

Context in Colossians 2:16-17:

  • Warning against legalistic judgments from others
  • These regulations were shadows of the reality to come
  • The reality is found in Christ
  • Freedom from human regulations and traditions
  • Emphasis on spiritual substance over external forms

What This Freedom Means:

  • No Condemnation: Free from others' critical judgments
  • Personal Liberty: Freedom in non-essential matters
  • Focus on Christ: Center faith on Jesus, not rules
  • Unity in Diversity: Different practices can coexist
  • Grace-Based Living: Not performance-based acceptance

Areas Mentioned Where We Shouldn't Be Judged:

  • Dietary Choices: What you eat and drink
  • Religious Festivals: Celebration of special days
  • New Moon Celebrations: Monthly observances
  • Sabbath Days: Weekly rest and worship

Biblical Balance in Christian Liberty:

  • Freedom with Responsibility: Don't use freedom to cause others to stumble
  • Love Over Liberty: Limit freedom for others' sake
  • Essential vs Non-essential: Distinguish between core doctrines and personal practices
  • Conscience Matters: Respect different levels of conviction
  • Unity in Essentials: Agreement on foundational truths

Practical Applications Today:

  • Worship Styles: Different music preferences are okay
  • Dietary Practices: Vegetarian vs non-vegetarian choices
  • Celebration of Holidays: Christmas, Easter observances
  • Church Traditions: Various denominational practices
  • Personal Devotions: Different prayer and study methods

Warning Against Two Extremes:

  • Legalistic Judgment: Condemning others for different practices
  • License: Using freedom as excuse for sin
  • Balance: Freedom within biblical boundaries
  • Love: The guiding principle in all matters

Key Insight: Christian freedom means we're liberated from human judgments about external religious practices while being responsible to use our liberty in ways that honor God and build up others. The focus shifts from rule-keeping to relationship with Christ who fulfilled all regulations and gives us true spiritual freedom.
