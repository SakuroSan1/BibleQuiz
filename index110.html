BIBLE QUIZ: TRUTH VS MYTHS

2 Timothy 4:3-4
30
According to 2 Timothy 4:4, the time comes when people will turn away from the truth and turn aside to:
Science
Myths
Traditions
Themselves

Correct Answer: Myths (2 Timothy 4:4)

2 Timothy 4:3-4 (NIV): "For the time will come when people will not put up with sound doctrine. Instead, to suit their own desires, they will gather around them a great number of teachers to say what their itching ears want to hear. They will turn their ears away from the truth and turn aside to myths."

The Warning Signs:

  • Intolerance for Truth: People reject sound doctrine (v.3)
  • Self-Centered Spirituality: Seek teachings that suit personal desires (v.3)
  • Teacher Shopping: Gather teachers who tell them what they want to hear (v.3)
  • Spiritual Deafness: Actively turn ears away from truth (v.4)
  • Myth Addiction: Embrace comforting lies over challenging truth (v.4)

Characteristics of "Itching Ears":

  • Desire for comfortable messages over convicting truth
  • Preference for entertainment over transformation
  • Seeking affirmation rather than correction
  • Choosing feelings over facts
  • Following popularity rather than principle

Modern Examples of Spiritual Myths:

  • "God wants you to be rich and never suffer" (Prosperity Gospel)
  • "All spiritual paths lead to God" (Religious Pluralism)
  • "Truth is whatever you believe it to be" (Relativism)
  • "You're basically good and don't need salvation" (Humanism)
  • "The Bible contains errors and isn't fully trustworthy" (Liberal Theology)

The Antidote to Myths:

  • Commitment to sound doctrine (2 Timothy 1:13)
  • Regular study of Scripture (2 Timothy 3:16-17)
  • Discernment through the Holy Spirit (1 John 4:1)
  • Accountability to biblical community (Hebrews 10:25)
  • Willingness to be corrected (Proverbs 12:1)

Practical Application: Examine what you're listening to - does it scratch itching ears or speak biblical truth?
