BIBLE QUIZ: PLEASING GOD WHO TESTS HEARTS

1 Thessalonians 2:4
30
According to 1 Thessalonians 2:4, we are not trying to please people but God, who tests our ______.
Hearts
Faith
Works
Motives

Correct Answer: Hearts (1 Thessalonians 2:4)

1 Thessalonians 2:4 (NIV): "We are not trying to please people but God, who tests our hearts."

The Priority of Pleasing God:

  1. Divine Focus: Our ultimate audience is God, not people
  2. Heart Examination: God looks beyond actions to motives
  3. Authentic Ministry: Sincere service without people-pleasing
  4. Eternal Perspective: God's approval matters more than human praise

Context in 1 Thessalonians 2:1-6:

  • Paul defends the sincerity of his ministry in Thessalonica
  • His preaching was not with error, impure motives, or trickery
  • He didn't use flattery or seek human praise
  • He wasn't a burden to them but cared like a nursing mother
  • He worked night and day to not be financially burdensome

What It Means That God Tests Our Hearts:

  • Complete Knowledge: God sees our deepest thoughts and intentions
  • Motive Examination: Why we do what we do matters to God
  • Authenticity Check: Reveals whether our faith is genuine
  • Purification Process: God's testing refines our character
  • Accountability: We answer to God for our inner life

Biblical Examples of God Testing Hearts:

  • David: "Search me, God, and know my heart" (Psalm 139:23)
  • Solomon: "Give your servant a discerning heart" (1 Kings 3:9)
  • Jeremiah: "The heart is deceitful above all things" (Jeremiah 17:9-10)
  • Peter: Jesus' question: "Do you love me?" (John 21:15-17)

Practical Applications:

  • Motive Check: Regularly examine why you serve and give
  • Fear of God: Value God's opinion more than human approval
  • Transparency: Live with nothing to hide before God
  • Heart Prayer: Ask God to purify your motives
  • Service Evaluation: Are you serving to be seen or to please God?

Benefits of Pleasing God Rather Than People:

  • Freedom: Released from the tyranny of others' opinions
  • Integrity: Consistency between public and private life
  • Peace: Security in God's unchanging approval
  • Effectiveness: Ministry that transforms rather than impresses
  • Eternal Reward: God's "well done" rather than human applause

Key Insight: When we live to please God who tests our hearts, we find freedom from people-pleasing and discover the joy of authentic service that flows from pure motives and brings glory to God alone.
