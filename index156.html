BIBLE QUIZ: OUR CITIZENSHIP IS IN HEAVEN

Philippians 3:20
30
According to Philippians 3:20, our citizenship is in ______.
Heaven
Jerusalem
The Church
God's Kingdom

Correct Answer: Heaven (Philippians 3:20)

Philippians 3:20 (NIV): "But our citizenship is in heaven. And we eagerly await a Savior from there, the Lord Jesus Christ..."

Heavenly Citizenship:

  1. Spiritual Identity: Our true home is in heaven
  2. Eternal Perspective: Living with future hope
  3. Dual Reality: On earth but belonging to heaven
  4. Christ-Centered: Connected to our Savior's return

Context in Philippians 3:17-21:

  • Paul contrasts earthly-minded and heavenly-minded people
  • Enemies of the cross have earthly desires
  • Believers' citizenship is in heaven
  • We await Jesus' return from heaven
  • He will transform our earthly bodies

What "Citizenship in Heaven" Means:

  • Legal Status: We are registered citizens of heaven
  • Loyalty: Our ultimate allegiance is to heaven
  • Rights & Privileges: Access to heavenly blessings
  • Responsibilities: Representing heaven on earth
  • Protection: Under heaven's jurisdiction and care

Biblical Significance of Citizenship:

  • Roman Context: Paul wrote to Roman colony where citizenship was prized
  • Heavenly Privileges: Better than Roman citizenship rights
  • Eternal Security: Cannot be revoked or lost
  • Identity Marker: Defines who we truly are

Practical Implications for Believers:

  • Different Values: Live by heaven's standards, not earth's
  • Eternal Perspective: Temporary troubles vs eternal glory
  • Mission Mindset: We are ambassadors for heaven
  • Hope in Suffering: This world is not our final home
  • Pure Living: Represent our heavenly King well

The Eager Expectation:

  • Savior's Return: Waiting for Jesus from heaven
  • Transformation: He will change our bodies
  • Glorification: Made like His glorious body
  • Final Redemption: Complete salvation at His coming

Contrast with Earthly Mindedness:

  • Enemies of Cross: Focus on earthly things
  • Their Destiny: Destruction
  • Their God: Their stomach (appetites)
  • Their Glory: Their shame
  • Their Mind: On earthly things

Modern Applications:

  • Cultural Engagement: In the world but not of it
  • Political Allegiance: Ultimate loyalty to God's kingdom
  • Material Possessions: Temporary stewardship, not ownership
  • Social Identity: Defined by Christ, not social status
  • Future Hope: Shapes present priorities and choices

Key Insight: Our heavenly citizenship transforms how we live on earth - we become ambassadors representing our true homeland, living with values and priorities that reflect our eternal destination rather than temporary earthly existence, all while eagerly awaiting our Savior's return to take us to our permanent home.
