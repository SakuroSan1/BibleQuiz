BIBLE QUIZ: WHEN DID WISE MEN VISIT?

Matthew 2:1-18
30
According to the biblical evidence in Matthew 2, approximately how long after Jesus' birth did the Wise Men (Magi) visit Him?
Immediately after birth (same night as shepherds)
About 40 days later (at His temple presentation)
Approximately 1-2 years after birth
When Jesus was 12 years old (at the temple)

Correct Answer: Approximately 1-2 years after birth

Key Bible Evidence:

1. Greek Terminology Distinction:

  • Luke 2:12: Shepherds find "a baby (βρέφος - brephos) wrapped in cloths and lying in a manger"
  • Matthew 2:11: Wise Men find "the young child (παιδίον - paidion) with Mary his mother"
  • Significance: "Brephos" = newborn infant; "Paidion" = young child/toddler

2. Herod's Age Calculation:

  • Matthew 2:7: Herod asked Wise Men "when the star had appeared"
  • Matthew 2:16: Herod ordered killing of boys "two years old and under"
  • Significance: Herod used star's appearance to estimate Jesus' age (1-2 years)

3. Location Difference:

  • Luke 2:7: Jesus laid in manger (stable) at birth
  • Matthew 2:11: Wise Men visit Jesus in a "house"
  • Significance: Family moved from temporary to permanent housing

4. Travel Time Considerations:

  • Distance: Wise Men came from "the East" (likely Persia/Babylon)
  • Journey: Over 1,000 miles by camel caravan
  • Time: Several months to a year for such travel
  • Preparation: Time to observe star, interpret meaning, plan journey

Complete Timeline of Jesus' Early Years:

  1. Birth Night: Shepherds visit stable in Bethlehem (Luke 2:8-20)
  2. 8th Day: Circumcision at temple (Luke 2:21)
  3. 40th Day: Presentation at temple - Simeon & Anna (Luke 2:22-38)
  4. Months Later: Return to Bethlehem, move to house
  5. 1-2 Years After Birth: Wise Men visit house in Bethlehem (Matthew 2:1-12)
  6. After Visit: Escape to Egypt (Matthew 2:13-15)
  7. After Herod's Death: Return to Nazareth (Matthew 2:19-23)
  8. Age 12: Visit temple with parents (Luke 2:41-52)

Why Christmas Nativity Scenes Are Inaccurate:

  • Combined Events: Show shepherds and wise men together
  • Wrong Location: Show wise men at stable instead of house
  • Wrong Time: Show all events happening on one night
  • Wrong Age: Show Jesus as newborn when wise men visited

Historical & Cultural Context:

  • Jewish Purification: 40-day period after childbirth (Leviticus 12)
  • Census Stay: Families often stayed with relatives after census
  • House Structure: Animals on lower level, family above
  • Travel Limitations: Newborns couldn't travel immediately

What Jesus Was Probably Like When Wise Men Arrived:

  • Walking: Could have been toddling (12-24 months)
  • Talking: Simple words and phrases
  • Recognizing: Could interact with visitors
  • Family Setting: Living in settled home environment

Herod's Evil Plan & Timeline:

  1. Wise Men arrive in Jerusalem asking about newborn king
  2. Herod consults religious leaders about Messiah's birthplace
  3. Herod sends Wise Men to Bethlehem with deceptive request
  4. Wise Men warned in dream not to return to Herod
  5. Joseph warned to flee to Egypt with family
  6. Herod realizes he's been tricked by Wise Men
  7. Herod orders massacre of Bethlehem boys 2 years and under
  8. Jesus' family stays in Egypt until Herod's death

Prophetic Fulfillments:

  • Jeremiah 31:15: "Rachel weeping for her children" fulfilled in massacre
  • Hosea 11:1: "Out of Egypt I called my son" fulfilled in return
  • Isaiah 60:3: "Nations will come to your light" - Gentile recognition
  • Psalm 72:10-11: Kings bringing gifts to Messiah

Theological Significance:

  • Progressive Revelation: Different groups recognized Messiah at different times
  • Gentile Inclusion: Wise Men as first Gentile worshippers
  • Universal Kingship: Jesus recognized by both Jews (shepherds) and Gentiles (wise men)
  • Sacrificial Foreshadowing: Myrrh gift pointed to Jesus' death

Key Insight: The Wise Men did NOT visit baby Jesus in the manger on Christmas night. They visited "the young child" Jesus approximately 1-2 years after His birth, in a house in Bethlehem. This timeline is supported by: 1) Greek terminology difference (paidion vs brephos), 2) Herod's command to kill boys 2 years and under, 3) the location change from stable to house, and 4) the travel time from the East. Understanding this correct timeline helps us appreciate the biblical narrative's accuracy and recognize how common Christmas traditions have conflated separate events.
