BIBLE QUIZ: EVERY KNEE SHALL BOW

Philippians 2:10-11
30
According to Philippians 2:10-11, at the name of Jesus every knee should bow, in heaven and on earth and under the earth, and every tongue acknowledge that Jesus Christ is ______.
Savior
Lord
King
Messiah

Correct Answer: Lord (Philippians 2:10-11)

Philippians 2:10-11 (NIV): "that at the name of Jesus every knee should bow, in heaven and on earth and under the earth, and every tongue acknowledge that Jesus Christ is Lord, to the glory of God the Father."

Universal Lordship of Christ:

  1. Cosmic Authority: All creation acknowledges His lordship
  2. Voluntary & Involuntary Worship: Some bow willingly, others by force
  3. Divine Glory: Brings glory to God the Father
  4. Prophetic Fulfillment: Fulfills Isaiah's prophecy

Context in Philippians 2:5-11:

  • Christ's humility and incarnation
  • His obedience to death on the cross
  • God exalted Him to the highest place
  • Given the name above every name
  • Universal worship and confession
  • Ultimate glory to God the Father

Three Realms of Worship:

  • Heaven: Angels and heavenly beings
  • Earth: All living humans
  • Under the Earth: Demons and departed souls
  • Universal Scope: No exceptions, all must bow

Significance of "Lord" (Kyrios):

  • Divine Title: Used for Yahweh in Greek Old Testament
  • Imperial Context: Caesar was called "Lord" in Roman world
  • Christian Confession: Earliest Christian creed "Jesus is Lord"
  • Absolute Authority: Supreme ruler over all creation

Old Testament Fulfillment:

  • Isaiah 45:23: "Before me every knee will bow"
  • Applied to Jesus: What Yahweh claims for Himself, Jesus receives
  • Divine Equality: Jesus shares divine honors with the Father
  • Prophetic Confirmation: Old Testament points to Christ's lordship

Practical Implications for Believers:

  • Present Submission: We bow willingly now
  • Evangelistic Urgency: Others will bow eventually
  • Worship Lifestyle: Every area of life under His lordship
  • Courage in Witness: Christ's ultimate victory is certain
  • Hope in Persecution: Even enemies will acknowledge Him

Theological Significance:

  • Christ's Deity: Only God receives such worship
  • Trinitarian Glory: Son's exaltation glorifies the Father
  • Eschatological Certainty: Final victory is guaranteed
  • Soteriological Necessity: Confession essential for salvation (Romans 10:9)

Modern Applications:

  • Cultural Engagement: Christ's lordship over all spheres
  • Personal Devotion: Daily submission to His authority
  • Corporate Worship: Gathering to honor Him as Lord
  • Ethical Living: Moral decisions under His lordship
  • Eternal Perspective: Living in light of final judgment

Key Insight: The universal confession of Jesus as Lord represents the culmination of God's redemptive plan - from Christ's ultimate humiliation on the cross to His supreme exaltation where every being in all creation willingly or unwillingly acknowledges His absolute sovereignty, bringing ultimate glory to God the Father who orchestrated this magnificent salvation.
