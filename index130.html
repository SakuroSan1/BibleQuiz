Correct Answer: Right (2 Thessalonians 3:13)

2 Thessalonians 3:13 (NIV): "And as for you, brothers and sisters, never tire of doing what is right."

The Call to Persevere in Righteousness:

  1. Steadfast Commitment: Continuous pursuit of moral excellence
  2. Counter-Cultural: Standing against idleness and wrong behavior
  3. Spiritual Endurance: Maintaining integrity when others compromise
  4. Faithful Witness: Demonstrating Christ-like character consistently

Context of 2 Thessalonians 3:6-15:

  • Paul addresses idleness in the Thessalonian church
  • Some were not working but being busybodies
  • Paul sets example of hard work and self-sufficiency
  • Command to work quietly and earn their own living
  • Verse 13 specifically encourages those who ARE doing right to continue

What "Doing Right" Includes:

  • Moral Integrity: Living according to God's standards
  • Hard Work: Being productive and responsible
  • Love in Action: Serving others selflessly
  • Faithful Stewardship: Using resources wisely
  • Truthfulness: Speaking and living honestly
  • Perseverance: Continuing when it's difficult

Why We Might Tire of Doing Right:

  • Seeing others get away with wrong behavior
  • Lack of immediate results or appreciation
  • Spiritual weariness and discouragement
  • Cultural pressure to compromise
  • Personal weakness and temptation

Biblical Encouragement for Perseverance:

  • Galatians 6:9: "Let us not become weary in doing good..."
  • 1 Corinthians 15:58: "Always give yourselves fully to the work of the Lord..."
  • Hebrews 12:1-3: "Run with perseverance the race marked out for us..."
  • James 1:12: "Blessed is the one who perseveres under trial..."

Practical Applications:

  • Daily Commitment: Start each day with resolve to do right
  • Accountability: Partner with other believers for encouragement
  • Spiritual Renewal: Regular prayer and Scripture reading for strength
  • Eternal Perspective: Remember God's ultimate reward and approval
  • Community Impact: Your consistency inspires others

Key Insight: Doing right is not about perfection but about persistent direction - consistently choosing God's way even when it's difficult, unpopular, or goes unnoticed by others.
