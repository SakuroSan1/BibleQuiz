BIBLE QUIZ: DEALING WITH DIVISIVENESS

Titus 3:10
30
According to Titus 3:10, how many times should you warn a divisive person before having nothing to do with them?
Once
Twice
Three times
Seven times

Correct Answer: Twice (Titus 3:10)

Titus 3:10-11 (NIV): "Warn a divisive person once, and then warn them a second time. After that, have nothing to do with them. You may be sure that such people are warped and sinful; they are self-condemned."

The Biblical Process for Dealing with Divisiveness:

  1. First Warning: Gentle correction with opportunity for repentance
  2. Second Warning: Firm admonition with clear consequences
  3. Separation: Avoidance after continued unrepentance

Why This Approach Matters:

  • Grace: Gives multiple opportunities for repentance
  • Truth: Protects the church from harmful influence
  • Balance: Both merciful and just
  • Church Protection: Prevents spread of false teaching and division

Biblical Context:

  • Similar to Jesus' teaching in Matthew 18:15-17
  • Paul addresses same issue in Romans 16:17-18
  • Purpose is restoration, not punishment (2 Thessalonians 3:14-15)

Practical Application:

  • Approach with humility and love initially
  • Be clear about biblical boundaries
  • Protect church unity while seeking restoration
  • Remember the goal is repentance and reconciliation

Important Note: This instruction is for persistent, unrepentant divisiveness, not minor disagreements or one-time offenses.
