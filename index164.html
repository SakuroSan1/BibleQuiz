BIBLE QUIZ: BE FILLED WITH THE SPIRIT

Ephesians 5:18
30
According to Ephesians 5:18, do not get drunk on wine, which leads to debauchery. Instead, be filled with the ______.
Holy Spirit
Word of God
Love of Christ
Peace of God

Correct Answer: Holy Spirit (Ephesians 5:18)

Ephesians 5:18 (NIV): "Do not get drunk on wine, which leads to debauchery. Instead, be filled with the Spirit."

The Divine Contrast:

  1. Negative Command: Avoid alcoholic intoxication
  2. Positive Command: Pursue spiritual fullness
  3. Alternative Control: Spirit-control vs alcohol-control
  4. Different Results: Debauchery vs righteousness

Context in Ephesians 5:15-21:

  • Live as wise people, not unwise
  • Make the most of every opportunity
  • Understand what the Lord's will is
  • Contrast: wine intoxication vs Spirit filling
  • Results of Spirit-filling: speaking, singing, thanksgiving, submission

What "Be Filled" Means:

  • Continuous Action: Present imperative - keep being filled
  • Passive Voice: Allow yourself to be filled by God
  • Complete Control: Like wine controls drunk person
  • Multiple Fillings: Not one-time event but ongoing experience
  • Communal Aspect: "You all" be filled - corporate experience

Contrast Between Wine and Spirit:

  • Wine: External substance leading to loss of control
  • Spirit: Internal presence leading to Christlike control
  • Drunkenness: Leads to debauchery (asōtia - unrestrained living)
  • Spirit-Filling: Leads to worship, gratitude, mutual submission

Four Results of Spirit-Filling (vs. 19-21):

  • Worshipful Speech: Speaking to one another with psalms, hymns, spiritual songs
  • Heart Song: Singing and making music from your heart to the Lord
  • Continual Thanksgiving: Always giving thanks to God for everything
  • Mutual Submission: Submitting to one another out of reverence for Christ

How to Be Filled with the Spirit:

  • Yieldedness: Surrender control to God
  • Obedience: Walking in God's will
  • Prayer: Asking for Spirit's fullness (Luke 11:13)
  • Word: Letting Christ's word dwell richly (Colossians 3:16)
  • Confession: Removing hindrances to Spirit's work

Historical Context:

  • Dionysus Cult: Worship involved drunkenness in Ephesus
  • Roman Culture: Drunkenness common at social gatherings
  • Christian Alternative: Spirit-empowered celebration instead
  • Contrast Witness: Christians marked by different "intoxication"

Modern Applications:

  • Addiction Recovery: Spirit's power over substance abuse
  • Worship Renewal: Spirit-inspired corporate worship
  • Relationship Health: Spirit-enabled mutual submission
  • Emotional Health: Spirit-produced joy and gratitude
  • Cultural Witness: Distinctive Christian lifestyle

Key Insight: Being filled with the Spirit is not about extraordinary emotional experiences but about daily surrender to God's control, resulting in a transformed life marked by worship, gratitude, and loving relationships - the exact opposite of the selfish indulgence that characterizes worldly intoxication.
